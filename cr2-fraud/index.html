Why This Proves Fraud

The Physics of Real Sensors

Camera sensors don't see images—they see photons. Photons are noisy, bouncing energy that generates electrical charges at each pixel. This charge is converted to 14-bit luminosity values (0-16,383). A real sensor always produces:

  • Shot noise — Random variation from photon counting
  • Thermal noise — Scales with sensor temperature (doubles every 6-8°C)
  • Read noise — Electronic noise from the ADC (typically 2-10 e- RMS)

Think of image formation like a wooden sculpture:

  • The Signal: The carved shape of the wood (the image you see)
  • The Noise: The natural grain of the wood (always present)

The "Dead Zone" Evidence

Real sensors generate "Dynamic Black"—a baseline of thermal and electrical noise at the far left of the histogram. In the Jonas files, that space is a Dead Zone: a complete vacuum of data between the black point and the image signal.

  • The Physics: An empty noise floor means there was no electrical activity in the sensor.
  • The Verdict: The camera did not take this picture. The image data was inserted into a hollow CR2 file.

Critical Forensic Failure

Scammers injected 'clean' digital images (created in Photoshop or VFX tools) into the CR2 file, forgetting that real hardware always writes sensor noise. The absence of this noise is the fingerprint of fraud.
